Destination Utrecht
Twelve days until my Erasmus year begins
20 August 2013 (published 26 April 2022)
4 min read

There are only twelve days to go until I up sticks and move to the Netherlands for my Erasmus year at Universiteit Utrecht. I don’t think I’ve ever switched between feeling excited and thinking “what have I gotten myself into” as much as I have over the last 48 hours, especially since waking up yesterday and realising that I’m leaving next week!

For the uninitiated, the Erasmus programme is an EU student exchange programme, allowing exchanges of between three months and one year. I’ll be spending the third year of my degree abroad, studying Earth Science (my degree is actually in Geophysics, but this isn’t offered at UU), with the modules I take counting towards my degree just as they would at home. You are also eligible for a grant on the Erasmus programme to contribute towards the costs associated with moving and living abroad, and for Brits, you pay no tuition fees while you are abroad.

Why Utrecht? I’ll be honest, while I’ve always wanted to study abroad since I knew it was a thing, I’d only ever really considered going to the US or Canada. When it came to choosing my year abroad options in my second year, I started looking into the options available to me in the US and Canada, and there was absolutely no way I could afford it. With no formal partnerships between my department and American/Canadian departments, I would be classed as a “visiting student” and would have to pay a substantial fee per course I took. Add to that the cost of flights, health insurance and accommodation, and there was no way I could make it work (I did the maths multiple times) and still be able to enjoy myself without having to work part-time, if that was even permitted on a student visa.

There were other options, though. I’d continued studying French as an extra-curricular at university, but while my everyday French was probably good enough to get by, I wasn’t confident that my French Geophysics vocabulary would be strong enough. I was finding the degree hard enough in English, let alone a language that I wasn’t fluent in! So, I resigned to not doing my third year abroad. I was disappointed, but consoled myself with knowing that there’d be other opportunities to study/work/live abroad in the future, perhaps when I had a little more disposable income.

One of my friends asked me if I’d considered studying at UU. I hadn’t - I’d assumed that I’d have to know Dutch, which I didn’t, but that turned out to not be the case! Part of the Erasmus programme, UU had a large number of exchange students, and most modules would be taught in English. Bonus, being part of Erasmus, I’d have no tuition fees and I’d be eligible for the Erasmus grant, taking away a good portion of my money worries for the year. I spoke to the person from my department currently on exchange at UU and I was sold. Utrecht looked beautiful and he’d had a fantastic year. The year abroad was back on!

Apart from a little bit of paperwork to post to UU, there hasn’t been too much preparation to do before going away. Being a well-established and tested exchange programme, Erasmus happily takes away most of the hard work. The most crucial part was putting together the Learning Agreement, which is essentially the list of modules I’ll be studying in Utrecht.

The moment it all became very real... posting off my paperwork to UU

My main concern at the moment is that I don’t know a single word of Dutch. Most people I’ve spoken to about my plans for next year have replied with “oh, so you can speak Dutch then?”. Actually, no, not a word. Ashamedly, I don’t even know how to say “thank you”.

I didn’t want to be that typical ignorant Brit abroad, so once everything was confirmed with my year abroad, I bought a book called Speak Dutch in 3 Months. I haven’t opened it. It’s in my wardrobe. The thought was there, but it’s too late now, and I’m hoping that my GCSE German and eight years worth of French will at least help me to pick up a little bit of Dutch.

Preparing for my big move is pretty much taking over my life right now, on top of finishing a summer placement at ZephIR Lidar in the Malvern Hills, planning a joint 21st birthday/leaving party and finishing off summer project work for my degree. It’s not lost on me that I moved from the hustle and bustle of London to the sleepy (at least in comparison) town of Malvern at the start of summer, and will now be trading the Malvern Hills for one of the flattest countries around.
